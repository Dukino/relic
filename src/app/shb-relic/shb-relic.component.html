Before You Begin...

Before you can begin the grind for your Resistance Weapons, you must complete the following quests for the Main Scenario Quests and Side Quests.

Main Scenario Quest Requirement Side Quest Requirement
Shadowbringers - Lvl 80 The City of Lost Angels - Lvl 70

The side quest "The City of Lost Angels" is part of a quest chain for the Alliance Raids for Stormblood. You must complete each side story quest for this questline that ends with "The City of Lost Angels" in order to have access to the Resistance Weapon quests.

Resistance Weapons (iLvl 485)

The first time you reach this part of the questline, you will take on the quest "Fire in the Forge" which only occurs once. This quest gives you the materials you need for your first Resistance Weapon for free. After you have completed this quest, the quest "Resistance is (Not) Futile" becomes available as a repeatable quest for other Resistance Weapons.

As you turn in your 15 Timeworn Artifacts you will be asked to allocate the stats of your weapon. These stats can be whatever you want them to be as long as you use the number required. If you aren't sure how to allocate your stats refer to the guide below. If you already know what stats you want please ignore the following.

Stat Allocation Suggestions

If you aren't sure how to allocate your stats and want a good rule of thumb to follow, then refer to the following info based on the role that your weapon is for:


DPS Jobs

Generally your stat priority should follow the following outline:
Critical Hit > Direct Hit > Determination > Skill Speed/Spell Speed

Keep in mind that different jobs may want different stats priorities. For example, if you play Black Mage, you may want to take Spell Speed in the place of Critical Hit depending on your playstyle.


Healer Jobs

Generally your stat priority should follow the following outline:
Critical Hit > Determination > Piety=Spell Speed

Keep in mind that your Piety and Spell Speed allocations are based on preference. If you want to go faster, choose Spell Speed. If you need more mana regeneration, choose Piety.


Tank Jobs

Generally your stat priority should follow the following outline:
Critical Hit > Determination > Tenacity=Skill Speed

Keep in mind that your Tenacity and Skill Speed allocations are based on preference. If you want to go faster, choose Skill Speed. If you want a general stat increase, choose Tenacity. Tenacity is a sort of strange stat because of how it scales so most people prefer to use Direct Hit as a stat. But because the Tank weapons don't allow you to allocate Direct Hit as a stat you have to choose something else.
